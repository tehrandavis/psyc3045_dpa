What to notice
  • Both panels show the same information — the flow function is the negative slope of the landscape: f(φ) = −dV/dφ.
  • Valleys in the landscape = zero crossings where the flow curve has negative slope.
  • Increase a relative to b (push the a slider right): watch the anti-phase valleys at ±180° flatten, then vanish.
  • When anti-phase disappears, the ball must roll to in-phase — that is the finger-wiggling transition you felt in lab.
