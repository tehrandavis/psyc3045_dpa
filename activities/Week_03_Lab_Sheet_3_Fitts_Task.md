## 1) Setup: The Fitts Tapping Task

You will perform **rapid alternating movements** between two target circles affected by the Ebbinghaus illusion.

**The Task:**
- Start with your finger/stylus on one target circle
- Move as quickly and accurately as possible to the other target circle
- Alternate back and forth between the two targets
- Complete **10 oscillations** (20 taps total: 10 on each target)

**The Illusion:**
- **Target A:** Central circle surrounded by **large circles** (looks smaller)
- **Target B:** Central circle surrounded by **small circles** (looks larger)
- **Reality:** Both central circles are **identical in size**

## 3) Procedure

**Materials:**
- Ebbinghaus display with two targets (A and B) separated by **20 cm** (center-to-center)
- Both central circles are **identical in size** (e.g., 3 cm diameter)
- Stylus or finger for tapping

**Instructions:**

1. **Starting Position:** Place your stylus/finger on Target A.
2. **Command "Go!":** Begin alternating as quickly and accurately as possible.
3. **Complete 10 oscillations** (A→B→A→B... for a total of 20 taps).
4. **Partner Records:**
   - **Movement Time (MT):** Total time to complete 10 oscillations (use stopwatch)
   - **Errors:** Number of taps that **miss** the target circle (count only clear misses)

**Repeat 3 trials.**

## 9) Connecting to Fitts' Law

**Fitts' Law (1954):** Movement Time = a + b × log₂(2A/W)

- **A** = Amplitude (distance to target)
- **W** = Width (size of target)
- **Index of Difficulty (ID)** = log₂(2A/W)
