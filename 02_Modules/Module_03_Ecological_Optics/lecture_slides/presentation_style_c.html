Speaker Notes

Lecture 04

Locomotion I

Empirical studies of Gibson’s rules for locomotion

Introduction

Modes of Locomotion

Visual perception orientates us to distal objects, providing essential ecological utility.

Foundation

Ecological Context

  • Structure in ambient energy arrays is information.
  • Detection is direct (no mental representation needed).
  • Optic flow fields are used for direct behavioral control.

Theory

Visual Guidance Rules

  • Stationarity: Minimize change in the optic flow.
  • Targeting: Place focus of expansion (FOE) on goal.
  • Direction: Shift FOE to change course.
  • Collision: Stabilize expansion rate.

Empirical Data

The Moving Room

Visual proprioception dominates mechanical signals during postural conflict.

Spatial Tuning

Peripheral vs. Central

Peripheral flow provides a more robust signal for egomotion and stance control.

Dynamics

Heading & Eye Movement

Heading angle judgments remain accurate even during smooth pursuit eye movements (Warren, 1988).

Navigation

Strategy Preference

Locomotion combines optic flow and egocentric strategies. Flow dominates as richness increases.

Safety

Time-to-Contact (Tau)

Specified by the inverse of the expansion rate (τ ~ 1/R). Direct, non-computational pickup of information.

Summary

Active Perception

Perception is not defined in the absence of activity. Control of locomotion and perception are functionally inseparable.
