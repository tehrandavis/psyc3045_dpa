Module 03: Ecological Optics

The Geometry of Information

Radiant vs. Ambient Light

Radiant Light (Physics)

Energy radiating from a source. Homogeneous. What stimulates the receptors.

Ambient Light (Ecology)

Light reflected and structured by the environment. Heterogeneous. What specifies the world.

Key Concept: We don't see light; we see by means of light.

Gibson's Rules for Visual Guidance

  • To be still: Minimize change in the optic flow (Nonflow).
  • To move forward: Produce global optic expansion (Outflow).
  • To move to a target: Place the Focus of Expansion (FOE) on the target.
  • To stop safely: Keep the rate of expansion (Tau) steady.

Central vs. Peripheral Flow

Rule 2: Heading Perception

Can we see where we are going?

Problem: Eye movements disrupt the FOE.
Result: We can still detect heading accurately (Warren, 1988).

Rule 3: Visual Control of Steering

The "Great Debate"

  • Hypothesis A (Optic Flow): Steer by keeping FOE on the goal.
  • Hypothesis B (Egocentric Direction): Steer by centering goal in body.

Adaptive Control Laws

It's not "Either/Or". We flexibly weight flow based on environmental structure.

  • Sparse World: We use Egocentric Direction (curved paths).
  • Textured World: We use Optic Flow (straight paths).

Rule 4: Time-to-Contact

Tau (τ) Theory

  • Indirect View: Calculate T = Distance / Speed. (Hard!)
  • Direct View: τ = 1 / Rate of Expansion. (Directly visible!)

Testing Tau

The Deflating Ball (Savelsbergh et al.)

  • Experiment: Catch a ball that deflates while approaching.
  • Physics: Distance decreases, Speed is constant.
  • Optics: Rate of expansion decreases (looks like it's slowing/retreating).
  • Result: People grasp LATE. They trust the optics (τ).

Beyond Timing

Collision Severity (Tau-dot)

We don't just see when (Tau); we see how hard (Tau-dot).

  • τ̇ ≥ -0.5: Safe stop (Soft collision).
  • τ̇ < -0.5: Crash (Hard collision).
